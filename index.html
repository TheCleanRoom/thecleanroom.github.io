Protocol C: A Study in Quantized Model Stability

John Augustine Young

December 2025

Abstract: This paper presents a novel approach to addressing numerical instability in heavily quantized mixture-of-experts architectures. Through the implementation of packet-switched attention routing and variance-aware gating mechanisms, we demonstrate significant improvements in model stability under 2-bit weight quantization constraints.

1. Introduction

The deployment of large language models in resource-constrained environments has become increasingly critical as computational demands scale exponentially. Quantization represents a promising avenue for reducing memory footprint and inference latency, yet aggressive bit-width reduction to 2-bit representations introduces fundamental challenges.

2. Methodology

We introduce Protocol C, a packet-switched attention mechanism that discretizes token processing into four routing categories based on semantic density and syntactic structure through energy-based surprise detection.
