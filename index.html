Protocol C: A Study in Quantized Model Stability

John Augustine Young

December 28, 2025

Abstract: This paper presents a novel approach to addressing numerical instability in heavily quantized mixture-of-experts architectures. Through the implementation of packet-switched attention routing and variance-aware gating mechanisms, we demonstrate significant improvements in model stability under 2-bit weight quantization constraints.

1. Introduction

The deployment of large language models in resource-constrained environments has become increasingly critical as computational demands scale exponentially. Quantization represents a promising avenue, yet aggressive reduction to 2-bit representations introduces fundamental challenges...

2. Methodology

We introduce Protocol C, a packet-switched attention mechanism that discretizes token processing into four routing categories. This allows the model to allocate resources adaptively, processing noise with minimal overhead.

3. Experimental Setup

The research was conducted over a four-day period at a university research facility.
